DETAILED ITINERARY
DAY 1, DELHI

Arrive in Delhi. On arrival, you will be greeted and assisted by our representative and transferred to the hotel. (Room will be available from 1400 hrs onwards)

Delhi - Delhi has been the seat of power of several empires for about a millennium. It has been destroyed and rebuilt many times, and interestingly, a number of its destroyers have also been its rulers. Delhi today is a potpourri of two distinct cultural lineages— Old Delhi and New Delhi. Juxtaposed against the immaculately planned New Delhi created by the British Raj, Old Delhi, which was once the capital of Islamic India, is a labyrinthine network of lanes and by lanes with crumbling havelis and formidable mosques.

On arrival in Delhi transfer to the hotel.

Overnight at the hotel.

DAY 2, DELHI

Today you will be picked up for a tour of Delhi including following:

Today experience The Grandeur of Shahjahanabad (Old Delhi) built on the Banks of River Yamuna – enjoy a Rickshaw Ride (Paddy cab) in old Delhi streets – you will ride on a rickshaw through the narrow streets of Old Delhi. Seeing the beautiful Digambar Jain Naya Mandir from outside, see the spice market called Khari Baoli (Asia’s Largest Spice Market), and Gurudwara Sis Ganj (sikh temple).

Also enjoy a Photo stop at the Red Fort (from outside) – Red Fort is the fifth Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan’s elegant citadel of red sandstone, built on the western bank of the river Yamuna. The Red sandstone walls of the massive Red Fort rise 33 meter above the clamour of Old Delhi, as a reminder of the magnificent power and pomp of the Mughals.

Raj Ghat – Raj Ghat is a memorial to Mahatma Gandhi. It is a black marble platform that marks the spot of Mahatma Gandhi’s cremation, on 31 January 1948, a day after his assassination. It is left open to the sky while an eternal flame burns perpetually at one end. It is located on the banks of the river Yamuna in Delhi on Ring Road officially known as Mahatma Gandhi Road.

Drive past the Govt. Buildings – New Delhi houses several government buildings and official residences, reminiscent of the British colonial architecture. Today we will drive past a few of them, like The Parliament House (designed by Baker and 173m in diameter), the Rashtrapati Bhawan (once the Viceroy’s residence), now the official residence of the President of India.
